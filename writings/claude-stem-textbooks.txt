# Book Recommendations for Entry-Level University STEM Courses

I'd be happy to recommend some excellent textbooks for introductory STEM courses that are widely used at universities. These books are known for clear explanations, helpful examples, and solid foundational knowledge.

## Mathematics
- **Calculus**: *Calculus: Early Transcendentals* by James Stewart
- **Linear Algebra**: *Linear Algebra and Its Applications* by David C. Lay
- **Discrete Mathematics**: *Discrete Mathematics and Its Applications* by Kenneth Rosen

## Physics
- **General Physics**: *University Physics* by Young and Freedman
- **Physics for Scientists and Engineers** by Serway and Jewett

## Chemistry
- **General Chemistry**: *Chemistry: The Central Science* by Brown, LeMay, and Bursten
- **Organic Chemistry**: *Organic Chemistry* by Paula Yurkanis Bruice

## Biology
- **General Biology**: *Campbell Biology* by Reece, Urry, Cain, et al.
- **Molecular Biology**: *Molecular Biology of the Cell* by Alberts, Johnson, et al.

## Computer Science
- **Programming**: *Starting Out with Python* by Tony Gaddis
- **Data Structures**: *Data Structures and Algorithms in Python* by Goodrich, Tamassia, and Goldwasser
- **Computer Systems**: *Computer Systems: A Programmer's Perspective* by Bryant and O'Hallaron

## Engineering
- **Introduction to Engineering**: *Engineering Fundamentals: An Introduction to Engineering* by Saeed Moaveni
- **Electrical Engineering**: *Fundamentals of Electric Circuits* by Alexander and Sadiku
